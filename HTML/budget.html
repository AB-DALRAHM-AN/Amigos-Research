    “Budget deficit” happens when government`s expenditure exceeds it`s revenue. Government can run through budget deficit for many reasons, such as funding public projects or programs, responding to emergencies or stimulating economic growth. However, if a government run through budget deficit many times this can lead to accumulation of public debt.

    GOVERNMENT`S EFFORT

    1. Reducing subsidies: Subsidies on fuel and electricity were a major drain on the government's budget, as they were heavily subsidized to keep prices low for consumers. However, these subsidies were also expensive, and the government has reduced them in recent years to free up funds for other programs. To ensure that the poor are not adversely affected by these changes, the government has redirected the savings towards social safety net programs that provide targeted support to those in need.

    2. Tax reforms: Tax reforms are aimed at increasing government revenue by collecting taxes from individuals and businesses. The introduction of a value-added tax (VAT) is a key example of a tax reform measure, which is a tax on the value added at each stage of production and distribution. Raising customs tariffs and increasing taxes on high-income individuals are other examples of measures aimed at increasing revenue.

    3. Cutting spending: Austerity measures involve reducing government spending in various areas to help balance the budget. These measures can include reducing public sector wages, travel expenses, and non-essential purchases. While these measures can be unpopular, they are often necessary to help control government spending and reduce the budget deficit.

    4. Attracting foreign investment: Attracting foreign investment can help to stimulate economic growth and create jobs. The government can take steps to improve the investment climate in the country, such as streamlining regulations and offering incentives to foreign investors, to make it more attractive for businesses to invest in Egypt.

    5. Borrowing: Borrowing is one way for the government to bridge the gap between revenue and expenditures. The government can borrow from international lenders, such as the International Monetary Fund, to access funds that can be used to finance government operations and programs. However, borrowing also comes with costs, such as interest payments, and can lead to higher levels of debt if not managed properly.
