    Balance of Payments Overview

    The Balance of Payments is a record of a country's international transactions, including imports, exports, investments, and transfers. It is divided into two main components:

    1- The current account.

    2- The capital account.

    The current account includes transactions related to trade in goods and services, income and transfers,The capital account includes transactions related to capital transfers and investments.

    Egypt's balance of payments from 2001 to 2021:

    before the revolution:

    Egypt's balance of payments has witnessed significant changes over the past two decades. In 2001, Egypt had a current account deficit of $1.6 billion and a capital account surplus of $5.5 billion. This was due to the high level of foreign investment in the country at that time. However, by 2011, when the January 25 revolution took place, Egypt's current account deficit increased to $11.2 billion, while the capital account surplus decreased to $1.5 billion. This is due to the decrease in foreign investment and the increase in imports.

    after the revolution:

    The years following the 2011 revolution were particularly challenging for Egypt's balance of payments, The country witnessed a significant drop in foreign investment, while the tourism sector, an important source of foreign exchange, was hit hard. As a result, Egypt's current account deficit widened to $18.8 billion in 2013.

    To address this problem, the government introduced a number of economic reforms, including:

    - the devaluation of the Egyptian pound

    - reduced energy support

    Introducing the value added tax on most products and commodities. The economic reforms implemented by the government helped improve Egypt's balance of payments. By 2018, the current account deficit had decreased to $6.8 billion, while the capital account surplus had increased to $9.1 billion. This was due to a combination of factors, including:

    -Tourism recovery

    - And increase foreign investment.

    In 2020 and like all countries of the world, Egypt's balance of payments has been affected by the COVID-19 pandemic.

    The state saw:

    A drop in tourism and a drop in exports, leading to a widening current account deficit. However, the government's efforts to attract foreign investment and implement economic reforms have helped mitigate the impact of the pandemic on the country's balance of payments.

    to summary all of this:

    Egypt's balance of payments has witnessed significant changes over the past two decades. The country has experienced both surpluses and deficits, depending on a combination of factors, including foreign investment, tourism, and the global economic environment.

    However, the government's efforts to attract foreign investment and implement economic reforms have helped improve Egypt's balance of payments.

    However, the COVID-19 pandemic has posed new challenges to the country's economic stability, highlighting the need for continued efforts to strengthen the balance of payments.
